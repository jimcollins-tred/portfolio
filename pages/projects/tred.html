Project / Spotlight

Home screen redesign

This year, we commissioned two UX designers to gather feedback on the app from our users. Based on their findings, I led a complete redesign of the app home screen, from concept to implementation.

The most common complaint was that the home screen was too busy and packed with information that wasn't required every time users opened the app. Listing open banking accounts was deemed distracting, and users felt the always-open transaction panel took up too much space.

The redesign displays information in a cleaner, less cluttered way. The balance is displayed clearly at the top, and open banking accounts are tidied away into their own menu that's easily accessible by tapping the account selector arrow.

Meanwhile, only the most recent transactions are displayed, with a simple button to bring up all transactions. Context-sensitive shortcut buttons also provide quick access to the features our users deem most important, such as PIN and account details. These shortcuts change depending on the type of account selected - Tred or open banking.

Finally this new home screen is designed to be modular. Additionally widgets can be added as required - such as the 'Green Money' widget shown above - with the screen now dynamically scrolling to accommodate this.

Animations

The new app also features various animations I implemented using a mixture of Rive, React Native Reanimated and CSS.

The opening animation on the left was designed as a fun, on-brand alternative to a loading spinner. The trees grow gradually as the app fetches required data during launch. I designed and implemented this animation using Rive, a tool that allows the creation of slick animations and their easy integration into frameworks like React Native.

On the right we have the header animation driven by the opening of the transaction drawer. The transaction drawer uses React Native Reanimated for the drawer open animation, which provides a precise Animated Value for the current open height of the drawer. I used this to synchronise the header transition animation with the opening of the drawer.
